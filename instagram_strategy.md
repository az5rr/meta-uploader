## Insights

### Short ayat vs long ayat
- Short ayat and short duaa are better suited to the required visual style because they preserve whitespace and readability
- Long ayat should be split across reel frames or kept for captions, not placed as dense centered text on a single screen

### Duaa vs Qur'an verses
- Duaa is easier to present in one-screen minimal compositions
- Qur'an verses can perform strongly for saves when the selection is brief, emotionally resonant, and visually easy to revisit
- The account should use both, but avoid long explanatory body text inside the visual itself

### Arabic vs Arabic with translation
- Arabic should remain primary in the visual
- A very short translation can be added in small type for selected posts and reels
- Translation should stay under one line when possible to preserve calm composition

### Save/share behavior patterns
These cannot be measured from the current data. Working hypothesis until analytics arrive:
- Saves are most likely to increase on concise ayat and duaa with direct personal relevance
- Shares are most likely to increase on universal themes: mercy, patience, tawakkul, forgiveness, light, and remembrance

## Strategy Update

### Non-negotiable direction
- Keep the pattern fixed: Reel -> Post -> Reel -> Reel -> Post -> Reel
- Remove off-theme informational/location content from the Qur'an account pipeline
- Prioritize Qur'an ayat and authentic duaa with clear emotional relevance
- Use black or white backgrounds with centered text only
- No hooks, logos, usernames, or branding inside the content

### Creative rules
- One primary Arabic text block per asset
- Optional translation only if it stays visually light
- Strong preference for short passages with tashkeel
- Use generous spacing and elegant typography
- Keep captions reflective and quiet, not explanatory or promotional
- Use fewer, more relevant hashtags

### Content mix going forward
- Reels: short ayah or duaa, 7 to 15 seconds, gentle motion only
- Posts: single-screen saveable verses or duaa with slightly larger Arabic text
- Maintain the repeating block structure: `(Reel -> Post -> Reel)`
- Avoid paragraph-length informational captions inside the visual

### Upload timing rules
- First reel slot: `06:00 Asia/Muscat` (`02:00 UTC`)
- Post slot: `09:00 Asia/Muscat` (`05:00 UTC`)
- Second reel slot: `20:00 Asia/Muscat` (`16:00 UTC`)
- The block is always: morning reel, morning post, evening reel
- When one block ends, the next block begins with the next available morning reel slot
- This naturally creates the visible sequence: `Reel -> Post -> Reel -> Reel -> Post -> Reel -> Reel -> Post -> Reel`

### Immediate correction
- Stop scheduling the repeated Makkah Clock reel concept for this account
- Replace future reels with scripture-centered or duaa-centered content matching the account identity
- Audit future scheduling so each live queue segment respects the repeating block: `(Reel -> Post -> Reel)`
